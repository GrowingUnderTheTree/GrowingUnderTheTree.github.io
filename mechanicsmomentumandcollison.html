Projectile Motion

What is Projectile Motions? (Definitions)

Projectile motion is The motions in 2 Dimensions

Projectile motion graphs usually shows Velocity & Displacement Physic Quantities, which will be resolved into two components, the horizontal(x) and Vertical(y) component.

Using the formula we learnt at Form 5 Physics Textbooks(Resultant Force), we can know that at the initial point of initial velocity can be break down to two components which is:

u sin θ, as velocity vertical component (y-axis)

and

u cos θ, as displacement horizontal component (x-axis)

Equation of Motion in a Horizontal Direction

Using the Linear Motion Equation to get Displacement, which is : $$ s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^{2} $$

Given Horizontal Displacement at any point, s = x, a = 0 ms-2 and ux = V0 cos θ ms-1,

We get that

$$ x=(V_{0} cos \theta)t $$

Which t will be the displacement and t will be time needed

Thus, time needed will be $$ t=\frac{x}{V_{0} cos \theta} $$

Given Horizontal Displacement at any point, s = y, a = -g(9.81) ms-2 and uy = V0 sin θ ms-1,

acceleration for y-axis will be constant due to free fall motion

Thus, $$ y=(V_{0}sin\theta)t-\frac{1}{2}(g)t^{2} $$

Substitute t: $$ t=\frac{x}{V_{0} cos \theta} $$

We will get $$ y=xtan\theta-\frac{gx^{2}}{2V_{0}^{2}cos\theta^{2}} $$ Which is called the Trajectory Equation

We could Derive Max range and Max Height based on the y component and x component

To get the Max Range(R), y will be at 0 meters and x axis will be R, and we will substitute into the Trajectory Equation $$ 0=Rtan\theta-\frac{gR^{2}}{2V_{0}^{2}cos\theta^{2}} $$

$$ Rtan\theta=\frac{gR^{2}}{2V_{0}^{2}cos\theta^{2}} $$

$$ R=\frac{gR^{2}}{2V_{0}^{2}cos\theta sin\theta} $$

$$ \frac{1}{R}=\frac{g}{2V_{0}^{2}cos\theta sin\theta} $$

$$ R=\frac{V_{0}^{2}sin2\theta}{g} $$

To get the Max Height, y axis will be named H meters and x axis will be Max Range over 2(0.5R), and we will substitute into the Trajectory Equation

$$ H=\frac{V_{0}^{2}sin^{2}\theta}{2g} $$
