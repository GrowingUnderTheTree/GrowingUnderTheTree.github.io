Projectile Motion

What is Projectile Motions? (Definitions)

Projectile motion is The motions in 2 Dimensions

Projectile motion graphs usually shows Velocity & Displacement Physic Quantities, which will be resolved into two components, the horizontal(x) and Vertical(y) component.

Using the formula we learnt at Form 5 Physics Textbooks(Resultant Force), we can know that at the initial point of initial velocity can be break down to two components which is:

u sin θ, as velocity vertical component (y-axis)

and

u cos θ, as displacement horizontal component (x-axis)

Equation of Motion in a Horizontal Direction

Using the Linear Motion Equation to get Displacement, which is : $$ s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^{2} $$

Given Horizontal Displacement at any point, s = x, a = 0 ms-2 and ux = V0 cos θ ms-1,

We get that

$$ x=(v_{0} cos \theta)t $$

Which t will be the displacement and t will be time needed
